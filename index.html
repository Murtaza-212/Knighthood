📖 YOUR GUIDE TO THE PATH OF KNIGHTHOOD

Everything you need to begin and sustain the three-year quest. Read this once, then use the scroll daily.

⚙ BEFORE YOU BEGIN — SET UP

Install the scroll on your phone. Open this file in Chrome (Android) or Safari (iPhone) → tap Share → Add to Home Screen. It installs as a standalone app with a sword icon. This is your home for 3 years — make it one tap away.

Set up cloud sync (optional but recommended). In the Daily Quest tab, tap 📜 BIND THE SCRIBE → follow the 4-step Firebase guide. Takes 10 minutes once. After that your data syncs automatically across phone and laptop.

Export a backup monthly. Tap ⬇ EXPORT SCROLL after your first week. Save to Google Drive. Do this on the 1st of every month — three years of data is worth protecting.

⚔ THE DAILY SYSTEM — ONE JOB

Every day has one job: tick your 5 daily oaths and press SEAL THE OATH before midnight.

⚔ TRAINING SESSION · 20 HONOUR
Any workout — strength, cycling, or mobility. Tick it done.
🦶 MARCH 8,000+ LEAGUES · 20 HONOUR
Steps on your phone pedometer. Build this into your commute.
🌿 LIMBERING RITE · 20 HONOUR
10+ minutes of stretching or mobility work. Non-negotiable.
☀ SUNLIGHT VIGIL · 20 HONOUR
15–20 minutes outdoors. Morning is best. Any time counts.
💧 THREE FLAGONS · 20 HONOUR

🌾 RANK I — THE CALL TO ARMS · DAYS 1–14

These first two weeks are not about performance. They are about showing up. The only trials are consistency ones: complete 10 of 14 sessions, march 5,000+ leagues on 7 days, complete the limbering rite on 10 days.

Your Day 1 workout (open the 📜 NOVICE TRIALS tab for the full plan):

• Warm-up: Tap the warm-up block above your missions → Start 5-min timer
• Incline push-ups: 3 sets to comfortable failure
• Glute bridges: 3 × 15 with 2-sec hold at top
• Bodyweight squats: 3 × 15 full depth
• Plank: Open ⏱ WAR TIMER → tap "Plank 20s" preset → hold it
• Cool-down: 5 min stretching, focus on hips and lower back

Do not attempt any rank trials in the first two weeks. Your only goal is the habit.

⚔ MONTHS 1–3 · RANK I → III

Follow the 📜 NOVICE TRIALS tab exactly. Every exercise has sets, reps, and tempo listed. The ⏱ WAR TIMER tab has a preset for every plank hold in the system — use it constantly.

The Rank II ceremony comes when: you have done 14+ training sessions, you can physically achieve 20 floor push-ups with a 3-second descent, DB row 10 lbs × 12, and Plank 45 sec. Tap the 🏰 RANK TRIAL button, check every box honestly, press CERTIFY.

Log your first session record in the 💪 BATTLE RECORDS tab after your first proper workout. Push-ups, plank time, weight. That number is your baseline. In 3 months you will barely recognise it.

🗡 MONTHS 3–6 · RANK III → IV · THE FORGE

Open the 📋 FORGE PHASE tab. This is where Phase 1 ends and real training begins. Goblet squats and Romanian deadlifts enter the programme. Feet-elevated push-ups replace floor push-ups. Interval cycling replaces steady cycling.

Thursday is your most important day. The restoration day includes wrist prep exercises — critical for Rank V and beyond. Do not skip it.

Take your first progress photo this month. The scroll reminds you every 28 days — tap 📸 TAKEN when done. In 6 months you will thank yourself for having that first photo.

🏹 MONTHS 6–12 · RANK IV → V · THE SQUIRE

The biggest jump in the system. The Squire rank requires movements you have not done before: archer push-ups and single-leg squats to a chair. The FORGE tab — Phase 4 walks through the progressions step by step.

Push progression: Wide push-up → side-shifted → archer negative → full archer
Squat progression: Split squat → single-leg to high chair → lower the chair each month

Do not tick the RANK TRIAL until you can perform the movement cleanly, not just a version of it. The realm demands honesty.

🏰 YEAR 1–2 · RANK V → VI · THE KNIGHT

Open FORGE — Phase 5. Pike push-ups and pseudo-planche enter now. Your cycling builds to 45-minute sustained sessions at R6.

By this point the simulation shows your body has changed substantially — approximately 9–10 kg lighter, 16 cm off the waist, 65 clean push-ups, 4-minute plank. The 📈 HONOUR CHART tab will show your full year of data. When you hit a bad week, look at the chart — you will see how rare the dips are compared to the overall trend.

The chart will show you something else: consistency is the most powerful force in the system. Not intensity. Not perfect days. Showing up.

👑 YEAR 2–3 · RANK VI → VII · THE LEGEND

Full pistol squats. 60-minute rides. Pseudo-planche push-ups. This is elite territory — the top few percent of any population, reached by ordinary daily commitment.

The simulation confirms this rank is achievable by the end of Year 3 for a consistent practitioner. The only warriors who do not reach it are the ones who stop. The system has no ceiling — only an oath that either holds or breaks.

📜 THE DAILY LAWS — FOR THREE YEARS
Sunday is the Sabbath. The scroll disables the workout mission automatically. Rest, walk, eat well, sleep early. Come back Monday sharper.
When you get injured, tap 🩹 INJURY immediately. Penalties pause for 14 days. Your streak is protected. The realm understands wounds.
When you miss 5+ days, the comeback bonus activates automatically — your first 3 days back earn double Honour. The system rewards return, not perfection.
When you travel, open the 🔄 PILGRIMAGE tab. It has a 20-minute hotel room workout requiring zero equipment. Your oath does not pause because you are in a different city.
During the summer heat (Dubai June–September), train indoors. Cycling and home strength work replace outdoor movement. The sun vigil counts even 5 minutes at dawn before it gets brutal.
⚔ THE THREE LAWS
I. Never negotiate with the first step.
Open the scroll, start the warm-up timer, and you have already won. 90% of training sessions that begin are completed. The decision is not "will I train today" — it is "will I open the scroll."
II. Log it or it did not happen.
The Battle Records tab is not optional. Watching your push-up count rise from 15 to 65 is the most motivating thing in the system. The number is the proof. The chart is the story.
III. The oath is for you alone.
No one is checking. No one is grading. Every rank trial requires you to physically pass the movement with clean form and honest self-certification. The realm demands it — but more importantly, you will know.

Complete all prescribed reps with perfect form in 2 consecutive sessions → increase next session.
WEIGHT JUMPS

CARDIO JUMPS
Add +5 minutes OR +1 resistance level, never both at once.
IF PAIN OCCURS
Reduce load by 20–30% and rebuild. Pain is a signal, not a barrier to push through.

🍖 FEAST & FAST

THE WARRIOR'S DIET — FEAST & DISCIPLINE
THE GOLDEN RULE: DISCIPLINED FEAST, NOT STARVATION

You don't need to count calories obsessively. A 300–500 calorie daily deficit through smarter food choices + increased movement is enough to lose 0.5–1 kg/week sustainably. This is the sweet spot for preserving muscle while losing fat.

THE 8 SACRED LAWS OF THE WARRIOR'S TABLE
  1. Protein first, every meal. Aim for 120–150g protein daily. Chicken, eggs, fish, Greek yogurt, lentils. Protein preserves muscle while you lose fat.
  2. Eat real food 80% of the time. If it comes from a plant, animal, or the ground, it belongs on your plate. Ultra-processed food is the primary enemy.
  3. Drink 3L water daily. Hunger is often thirst. Water also improves energy, joint lubrication, and training performance. See the Lifestyle tab for the full hydration schedule.
  4. No liquid calories. Cut sodas, juices, and energy drinks completely. They add 200–500 calories of pure sugar with zero satiety.
  5. Eat carbs around training. Have your larger carb portions before or after workouts. Brown rice, oats, sweet potato, banana.
  6. Don't skip breakfast. A protein-rich breakfast reduces cravings all day. 3 eggs + 1 slice whole grain toast is a perfect start.
  7. Limit but don't ban treats. One cheat meal per week is fine. Restricting everything leads to binging. Build a lifestyle, not a prison.
  8. Sleep 7–9 hours. Poor sleep spikes ghrelin (hunger hormone) by up to 30%. You can't out-train a sleep-deprived body.
THE ROLE OF ENDURANCE IN BATTLE-READINESS
CYCLING SESSIONS
2× per week of 25–35 min cycling burns 250–400 calories/session. That's 500–800 cal/week from cardio alone.
DAILY MOVEMENT
8,000 steps/day adds ~300–500 calories burned vs. sedentary baseline. Steps are your silent fat burner.
STRENGTH TRAINING
Builds muscle. Muscle burns more calories at rest (NEAT effect). Every kg of muscle added burns 50–80 extra cal/day.
COMBINED EFFECT
Training + 8K steps + diet discipline = 0.5–1 kg fat loss per week. Sustainable, healthy, compounding.

🧠 MIND & BLADE
▼
THE WARRIOR'S MIND — CONNECTING WILL TO BLADE
THE PROBLEM — SLEEPING MUSCLES

After 10 years of inactivity, your brain has partially disconnected from key muscles — especially the glutes, core, and upper back. This is called altered motor recruitment. You can do 30 squats without your glutes firing properly, letting your lower back and knees compensate — which is exactly why you have pain. The fix is deliberate, slow activation work.

AWAKENING RITES — BEFORE EVERY TRIAL
🍑 GLUTES (Most Important)
Exercise: Glute Bridge — lie on back, feet flat, drive hips up. At the TOP, squeeze your glutes as hard as you can and hold 2 seconds. Imagine you're trying to crack a walnut between your cheeks.
TEST: If you feel this in your hamstrings more than glutes → feet too far forward. If lower back → hips too high. Adjust until you feel the burn directly in the glutes.
🏛️ CORE / TVA (Deep Stabilizer)
Exercise: Dead Bug — on your back, arms up, knees 90°. Before moving, draw your belly button toward your spine (not suck in — pull in and up). Hold this tension the ENTIRE exercise. If you lose it, stop and reset.
TEST: Place one hand under your lower back. There should be slight pressure from your back against your hand throughout. If the gap opens when you move a limb — your TVA released. Start the rep again.
🦋 UPPER BACK / SCAPULAR RETRACTORS
Exercise: Dumbbell Row — before pulling the weight, think "pull your shoulder blade into your back pocket." The arm is just a hook. The back does the work. Hold the top position 1 second and feel the squeeze between your shoulder blades.
TEST: If your bicep burns more than your back → you're pulling with the arm. Reset, lighter weight, focus on the shoulder blade movement first.
🦵 QUADS (Knee Stabilizers)
Exercise: Slow Bodyweight Squat — on the way DOWN (eccentric), actively push your knees out (don't let them cave in) and feel the quads loading. Think "spread the floor with your feet." At the bottom, pause 1 second before coming up.
TEST: If knees drift inward during the squat → glutes and quads not firing. Reduce depth until the pattern is clean.
🌊 LATS (Upper Back Stability)
Exercise: Before any push-up or press, "pack" your shoulders — pull them down and back away from your ears. Imagine tucking your shoulder blades into your back pockets. Hold this through the entire movement.
TEST: Shrug your shoulders up to your ears, then actively pull them down. That "down" position is what you should maintain during all pressing movements.
THE DELIBERATE STRIKE — TEMPO MASTERY
WEEKS 1–4: USE THIS TEMPO FOR EVERY LIFT
ECCENTRIC (lowering)
3 seconds — slow and controlled. This is where muscle is built and connection is made.
PAUSE (bottom)
1 second — pause at the hardest point. No bouncing. Forces true muscle activation.
CONCENTRIC (lifting)
1–2 seconds — deliberate, not explosive. Think about the muscle squeezing.
WHY THIS WORKS
Slow reps rebuild the neural pathway between brain and muscle. After 4–6 weeks your body "remembers" how to use these muscles automatically.

📖 SAGE'S COUNSEL
▼
THE SAGE'S COUNSEL — COMMON TRIALS

Click any question to expand the answer.

PAINMy knees still hurt after 2 weeks. What do I do?▼
This is common and expected in the first few weeks. First: identify if it's joint pain (inside the knee) or muscle pain (above/below the kneecap). Joint pain = stop all knee-loading exercises and switch to cycling only (very low impact) for 5–7 days. Muscle soreness = continue but reduce depth of squats to only 45° bend. In both cases: add extra glute bridge and calf raise work daily — weak glutes are almost always the root cause of knee pain. If pain persists beyond 3 weeks without improvement, see a physiotherapist. Do not push through joint pain.
PAINMy lower back hurts during and after workouts.▼
Lower back pain during exercise usually means one of three things: (1) Your core isn't bracing before the movement — practice diaphragmatic breathing and brace before every rep. (2) Your glutes aren't activating — do 2 sets of glute bridges before every session as a strict warm-up requirement. (3) Your hips are tight — the hip flexors are pulling your pelvis forward, stressing the lumbar spine. Add the couch stretch and 90/90 hip stretch to your daily mobility work. Remove all sit-ups and replace with dead bugs and bird dogs immediately. If pain is sharp or radiates down the leg — stop and see a doctor.
FAT LOSSI'm not losing weight after 3 weeks. What's wrong?▼
Three possibilities: (1) You're eating more than you think — liquid calories (juice, soda, coffee with sugar, energy drinks) are the most common hidden saboteur. Track everything you drink for 3 days. (2) You're losing fat but gaining muscle simultaneously — the scale won't move but your measurements will. Take waist measurement and progress photos — these don't lie. (3) Your deficit isn't large enough — check that you're not rewarding workouts with extra food. A 45-minute cycling session burns roughly 300 calories. One protein bar is 200. The margin is smaller than people think. Stay consistent with the nutrition rules for 6 weeks before judging results.
CONSISTENCYI missed 3 days in a row. Do I lose all my progress?▼
No. 3 days changes almost nothing physiologically. What it CAN do is break the psychological momentum — which is the real danger. The fix is immediate: don't try to compensate by doubling up sessions. Just return to the exact workout you would have done next and continue as normal. Every missed day you spend feeling guilty is a day you're not spending building. The system has no judgment. Log a lower XP week, then get back to work. One missed week in 3 months is a 97% consistency rate. That's excellent.
CONSISTENCYI feel too tired and sore to train today. Should I push through?▼
Distinguish between two types of tired: (1) Mental resistance / laziness — you're fine physically but your brain doesn't want to start. Solution: commit to just the warm-up. 95% of the time, once you're moving you'll complete the session. (2) Genuine physical fatigue / excessive DOMS — your muscles are deeply sore and heavy. Solution: switch to a mobility-only session. 30 minutes of stretching and light movement earns XP, reduces soreness, and keeps the habit alive without the risk of injury from training depleted. Never train through sharp pain. Always train through mild discomfort and mental resistance.
PROGRESSI can't progress on an exercise — stuck at the same reps for weeks.▼
A plateau in reps usually means one of: (1) Sleep is insufficient — muscle adaptation happens during sleep, not training. Under 7 hours consistently = minimal progress regardless of effort. (2) Protein is too low — without enough protein, your body can't rebuild the muscle fibers. Check you're hitting 120g+ daily. (3) You're rushing reps for the count — slow down to the 3-1-1 tempo and your effective reps increase dramatically. (4) Deload needed — if you've been training 5–6 weeks straight without a deload week, your nervous system is fatigued. Take a deload week and you'll often set a new personal record the following week.
MOTIVATIONI've lost motivation. I don't feel like doing this anymore.▼
This is the most important moment of the entire journey. Motivation is a feeling — feelings change. Systems don't. The entire XP framework exists for exactly this moment. You don't need to want to train. You need to complete the mission. Three things that reliably restore momentum: (1) Look back at your logs — compare what you could do on Day 1 to now. Progress is motivating. (2) Lower the bar temporarily — commit to 20 minutes instead of 60. Starting is the hardest part. (3) Change the environment — train at a different time, take your cycling outside if possible, play new music. Novelty re-engages the brain. The identity shift ("I am someone who trains") matters more than the motivation feeling. Act from identity, not emotion.
NUTRITIONI'm always hungry. How do I manage cravings?▼
Hunger management is 80% protein and water. Strategy: (1) Start every meal with your protein source — it triggers satiety hormones faster. (2) Drink 500ml of water before meals — genuine hunger is often thirst. (3) Add volume foods: cucumber, lettuce, zucchini, broth-based soups — high bulk, very low calorie, kills the sensation of emptiness. (4) If cravings hit at night: this is usually a signal that daytime meals weren't protein-dense enough. Fix breakfast and lunch, and evening cravings decrease dramatically. (5) Allow one "free meal" per week — this prevents the binge-restrict cycle that derails most diets.
EQUIPMENTCan I progress further once I've mastered the 30 lb dumbbell?▼
Yes — and this is a good problem to have. Options when you've outgrown the dumbbells: (1) Increase time under tension — slower reps (5 sec down, 2 sec hold, 2 sec up) make 30 lbs feel like 50. (2) Unilateral variations — single-leg Romanian deadlift, single-arm press — doubles effective difficulty per side. (3) Isometric holds — hold the hardest position (bottom of squat, top of row) for 3–5 seconds per rep. (4) Increase volume — more sets before increasing weight. 5 × 12 at 30 lbs is significantly harder than 3 × 12. (5) When ready: invest in heavier dumbbells (40–50 lbs) — by this point, the investment is well worth it.
POSTUREMy posture hasn't improved much after a month. Is something wrong?▼
Posture correction takes 2–4 months of consistent effort — it involves re-educating muscles that have been in the wrong position for 10 years. Check these three things: (1) Are you doing rows at least 2× per week? The upper back muscles (rhomboids and mid-traps) are the primary posture muscles — they need consistent direct work. (2) Are you doing the chin tuck exercise daily? This is the single most effective exercise for forward head posture. (3) Are you taking posture breaks at your desk every 45 minutes? 8 hours of desk slumping undoes 1 hour of gym work. The desk habits are AS important as the training. If doing all three consistently, you will see results by month 2.
